About Me

I was not a bright child. But something changed in class 7 when I discovered that excelling in studies was not as difficult as it seemed. Little did I know I was preparing to meet my first spiritual teacher.

In class 6, I wrote an essay about the need to know "that one thing" — the fundamental reality that weaves through everything in this universe. Just as a potter knows everything made of clay by knowing clay itself, I wrote about knowing that one thing which would reveal everything there is to know. This longing stayed with me.

When I was in class 8, Mr. Deepak Auddy joined our school as Maths and English teacher. He would inspire us with stories of Swami Vivekananda and Gautama Buddha, urging us to become ideals for our juniors whenever he had time after finishing the syllabus. Once, he gave us Meditation as a topic for speech preparation. Hearing the speeches prepared by other students sparked my interest. I went to the staff room to speak with him about all these profound questions.

He gave me a biography of Swami Vivekananda by Swami Nikhilananda. That was the turning point. I read the book cover to cover and was deeply inspired. I learnt meditation with him, and my sadhana began.
