Vedanta

Vedanta is one of the six systems of Indian philosophy. The word Vedanta literally means "the end of the Vedas" and refers to the philosophical teachings found in the Upanishads.

What is Vedanta?

Vedanta teaches the fundamental truth that:

  • The individual self (Atman) is identical with the ultimate reality (Brahman)
  • What we perceive as the world is ultimately an illusion (Maya)
  • Liberation (Moksha) comes from realizing this truth
