Starting a meditation practice can feel overwhelming, especially when you first sit down and realize how active your mind actually is. The good news is that you don't need any special equipment, expensive classes, or years of experience to begin. All you need is a few minutes and willingness to try.

## Why Start with Just Five Minutes

When learning how to meditate, less is more. Five minutes feels manageable, and that's exactly what makes it sustainable. You're not trying to empty your mind or achieve some special state. You're simply sitting still and paying attention to your breath.

The breath is always with you, making it the perfect anchor for meditation. When your mind wanders—and it will—gently bring your attention back to the sensation of breathing. This is not failure. This is the actual practice.

## Finding Your Time and Space

Most people find morning works best, before the day's responsibilities take over. But meditation can happen at any time. What matters is consistency, not perfection.

You don't need a special room or cushion. A quiet corner with a comfortable chair works fine. The goal isn't to create a perfect environment—it's to show up, even when life is busy.

## What to Do When You Sit Down

Here's a simple meditation you can try right now:

1. Sit comfortably with your back straight but not rigid
2. Close your eyes or soften your gaze
3. Notice your breath without trying to control it
4. When your mind wanders, gently return to the breath
5. Continue for your chosen time—start with five minutes

## Building a Sustainable Routine

The secret to a lasting meditation habit is starting so small that it's almost impossible to skip. Five minutes a day is far more valuable than an hour once a week.

Don't worry about whether you're doing it "right." Meditation is not about achieving a particular state. It's about paying attention to what's happening, moment by moment.
